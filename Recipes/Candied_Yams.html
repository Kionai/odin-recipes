Candied Yams!!

Move over sweet potatoe casserole! These candied yams are a holiday favorite!

Southern Candied Yams is by far my favorite side for holiday dinner. The recipe I'm going to share with you today will be, by far, the star of your holiday dinner ensemble! Let's get started!

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs sweet potatoes
  • 4 tbl unsalted butter
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup buttermilk
  • 1-2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/8 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp maple extract
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/8 tsp rum extract
  • ground clove, for sprinling ** sprinkle, sprinkle

Instructions

  1. Preheat over to 350 F
  2. Peel sweet potatoes and cut them into 1-inch , bite-sized pieces
  3. Place the sweet potatoes in a steamer basket once the water starts boiling. Cover and steam for about 7-10 minutes. (You want to steam just enough to soften them slightly. You don't want to cook them all the way)
  4. Once steamed, place them in a casserole dish. Set aside.
  5. To make the sauce, in a saucepan over medium heat, melt the butter
  6. Stir in the sugar and buttermilk
  7. Stir in the cinnamon, nutmeg and ginger until combined
  8. Cook until the syrup comes to a full simmer while stirring occasionally and then remove from heat.
  9. Stir in baking soda
  10. Stir in maple, vanilla and rum extract
  11. Pour the syrup over the potatoes and toss to coat
  12. Cover with aluminum foil and bake for 30-40 minutes or until tender.
  13. Remove from oven and toss
  14. Sprinkle on the ground clove and serve immediately
  15. Enjoy!
